How the pandemic changed my life

  1. Home
  2. My Journey

The pandemic has been an incredibly stressful time for most of us. It has taken a toll on our mental and physical health, and left many of us unemployed.

However, the pandemic helped me gain a different perspective on life. I managed to break into the data science industry in the midst of a Covid-19 lockdown.

Background

Since I was young, I have always enjoyed to solve puzzles.So thats how I look at big datasets: to me it is big puzzle I want to solve. Finding patterns nobody else sees is the challenge to me.Before the pandemic started, I was living a relatively comfortable life.I was enjoying my college.

All this changed when the government imposed a nationwide lockdown first time on 24 March,2020. Suddenly, I could no longer do the things I liked anymore. I could no longer go out with friends or go to the college everyday. I was stuck at home with nothing to do. The upside of all of this–now that I had nothing to do, I could think clearly. I started thinking about the future. About what I wanted to do, and who I wanted to become. I took a piece of paper and started making a list of the different career options I had:

  • Professor
  • Data scientist
  • Journalist

I watched videos and read articles on all of these career choices. I took a look at salaries, prospects, and job satisfaction. And machine learning really stood out to me. I started watching videos of AI applications. I watched, fascinated, as Watson (an AI application) beat the world champion at a game of Jeopardy. That was when I decided I was going to give it a try. I wanted to be part of the magic that created Watson. I wanted to pursue machine learning and work with data.

So I enrolled into data science online courses on Internet, and started doing challenges on sites like W3resource,JavaPoint,GeeksforGeeks,Towards data science,etc. It was difficult. But I had an end goal in mind. And I decided to keep trying until I became really good at it.

As I coded for 6 – 7 hours a day, I started to really enjoy it. I realized that my biggest barrier was my own fear. After getting over the initial fear that was holding me back, the rest fell into place pretty easily.

In just a few months, I:

  • Taught myself Python
  • Learnt the basics of data science and machine learning
  • Built machine learning projects

Not long after, my hard work paid off. I got an internship offer from a Codebiosis Private Limited company, Bengaluru. They were impressed with my projects. I was still studying at that time, and this was a full time internship opportunity. This meant that I had to juggle full-time studying with full-time working. But this wasn′t an opportunity you′d get everyday, so I said yes.

Then, six Months later, I started working in the same company as a data scientist.

In life, things happen to you that you don′t expect. But sometimes, those things happen for the best.
